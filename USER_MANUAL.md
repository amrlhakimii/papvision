## 1. What is PapVision?

PapVision is an interactive web-based learning platform designed to help students develop competency in Pap smear cytopathology. It combines high-resolution microscopy images, guided morphology annotations, and diagnostic quizzes to reinforce visual pattern recognition and diagnostic reasoning.

**Target users:** Cytopathology students, MLT students, and pathology trainees.

**Access:** Open your browser and go to **[papvision.my](https://papvision.my)**

PapVision works on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Logging In

No registration is required. PapVision uses Google Sign-In.

1. Go to **papvision.my**
2. Select **Student**
3. Click **Continue as Student**
4. A Google sign-in popup will appear — select or enter your Google account
5. You will be signed in automatically

Your progress, quiz scores, and achievements are saved to your account and will be restored every time you log in.

> **Note:** Use a personal or university Google account. Any valid Google account works.

### 2.2 Logging Out

Click the **menu icon** in the sidebar and select **Log Out**.

## 4. Learning Module

Navigate to **Learn** from the sidebar.

### 4.1 Categories

Learning content is organised into five categories, unlocked progressively:

| # | Category | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Normal Cervical Cells** | Superficial, intermediate, parabasal, columnar, and metaplastic cells |
| 2 | **Infectious Organisms** | Trichomonas, Candida, HSV, bacterial vaginosis, Actinomyces |
| 3 | **Benign Cellular Changes** | Reactive inflammation, radiation effect, IUD changes, atrophy |
| 4 | **Squamous Abnormalities** | ASC-US, ASC-H, LSIL, HSIL, Squamous Cell Carcinoma |
| 5 | **Glandular Abnormalities** | Normal endometrial cells, AGC, AIS, Adenocarcinoma |

> **Category unlocking rules:**
> - Normal and Infectious Organisms are available from the start.
> - Benign Cellular Changes unlocks after Normal + Infectious Organisms average ≥ 80%.
> - Squamous Abnormalities unlocks when Benign ≥ 60%.
> - Glandular Abnormalities unlocks when Squamous is unlocked and Benign ≥ 60%.

### 4.2 Viewing a Slide

Click any slide thumbnail to open the full learning view. Each slide contains:

- **High-resolution Pap smear image**
- **Cellular features** — morphology of the cell cytoplasm and borders
- **Nuclear features** — size, chromatin pattern, N/C ratio
- **Background features** — smear background observations
- **Diagnostic notes** — interpretation and key differentials
- **Clinical relevance** — what this finding means clinically

### 4.3 Interactive Morphology Cues

Numbered markers appear directly on the image. Tap or click any marker to open a popup explanation describing the exact morphological feature at that location.

**How to use:**
1. Open a slide
2. Look for the numbered circular markers on the image
3. Click a marker to read its morphology explanation
4. Close the popup by clicking outside it or pressing the X button

This feature is designed to train your eye to locate and name specific cytological findings on the image itself.

### 4.4 Zoomable Image Viewer

You can inspect cytological details at higher magnification:

| Action | Desktop | Mobile |
|---|---|---|
| Zoom in | Click **+** button or scroll up | Pinch to zoom |
| Zoom out | Click **−** button or scroll down | Pinch to zoom |
| Pan | Click and drag | Drag with one finger |
| Reset | Click the reset button | Double-tap |

> **Note:** Morphology cue markers remain accessible while zoomed in.

### 4.5 Completing a Slide

A slide is marked as completed once you open and view it. Progress is saved automatically.

## 5. Diagnostic Quiz

Navigate to **Quiz** from the sidebar.

### 5.1 Starting a Quiz

1. Select a **category** from the dropdown (or choose All Categories)
2. Select a **difficulty** level: Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced
3. Click **Start Quiz**

The quiz contains up to 16 questions drawn from the selected category and difficulty.

### 5.2 Answering Questions

Each question presents a clinical scenario or morphological description with four answer choices.

- Click your chosen answer
- Immediate feedback is shown after each answer

### 5.3 Feedback System

After each answer you will see:

| Feedback Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Correct / Incorrect** | Whether your answer was right |
| **Explanation** | Why the correct answer is right |
| **Key features** | The morphological features to look for |
| **Hint** | A guided clue (shown especially on incorrect answers) |
| **Clinical relevance** | The clinical significance of this diagnosis |

The system does not simply show "wrong — correct answer is X." It guides you to reason through the morphology.

### 5.4 Quiz Results

At the end of the quiz you will see:

- **Accuracy %** — percentage of correct answers
- **Correct answers** — count
- **Incorrect answers** — count

A score of **≥80%** is recorded as a pass for that category and difficulty level. Your highest score per category is always saved.

## 6. Challenge Mode

Navigate to **Challenge** from the sidebar.

Challenge Mode is a gamified practice environment that simulates real screening pressure. There are three modes:

### 6.1 Timed Quiz

- A **10-minute countdown** timer applies to the entire session
- Answer all cases before time runs out
- Accuracy and completion are both tracked
- Full feedback is shown after each answer before proceeding

**Best for:** Simulating a timed practical examination.

### 6.2 Rapid Screening

- **10 seconds per case** — the timer resets for each question
- The case auto-progresses when time runs out (counts as incorrect)
- No detailed feedback between questions
- Tests instant pattern recognition

**Best for:** Training rapid visual interpretation under time pressure.

### 6.3 Randomized Cases

- Questions are drawn randomly across beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels
- No time pressure
- Full feedback shown after each answer

**Best for:** Mixed revision across all difficulty levels.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: My progress disappeared after I logged out.**
A: Progress is tied to your Google account. Make sure you are signing in with the same Google account you used previously.

**Q: Some categories are locked. How do I unlock them?**
A: Categories unlock based on your quiz performance in earlier categories. Focus on Normal and Infectious Organisms first and achieve a combined average of ≥80% to unlock Benign Cellular Changes.

**Q: Can I retake a quiz?**
A: Yes. You can attempt a quiz as many times as you like. Only your highest score is recorded.

**Q: The image looks blurry when I zoom in. Is that normal?**
A: Slight pixelation at maximum zoom is expected due to the source image resolution. The zoom level is sufficient to observe the key cytological features described in each slide.

**Q: Does PapVision work on a phone?**
A: Yes. PapVision is fully responsive and works on desktop, tablet, and smartphone browsers. For the best experience with image zoom and cue markers, a tablet or desktop is recommended.

## 9. Accessing the Admin Panel

### 9.1 Signing In as Admin

1. Go to **papvision.my**
2. Select **Admin** (the dark panel on the login screen)
3. Click **Continue as Admin**
4. Sign in with the Google account that has been granted admin access

If your account does not have admin access, you will see an error message. Admin access must be granted by the developer before first use.

### 9.2 Admin Navigation

The admin panel has four sections accessible from the left sidebar:

| Section | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Overview stats, data seeding, and user list |
| **Slides** | Add, edit, and delete learning slides |
| **Questions** | Add, edit, and delete quiz questions |
| **Users** | View all registered students and their progress |

## 10. Admin Dashboard

The Dashboard gives an at-a-glance overview of the platform.

### 10.1 Stats

| Stat | Description |
|---|---|
| **Registered Users** | Total number of students who have signed in |
| **Learning Slides** | Total slides in the system |
| **Quiz Questions** | Total questions in the question bank |

### 10.2 Recent Users

A table at the bottom of the dashboard lists the most recently registered students, showing their name, email, role, and join date.

### 10.3 Seed Firestore

The **Seed Data** button pushes all slide and question content from the application code into the Firestore database.

**When to use this:**
- After deploying a new version of the app that includes updated slides or questions
- If content appears missing or broken for students

> This operation is safe to run multiple times. It overwrites existing records by ID and does not delete anything.

### 10.4 Verify Data

The **Verify Now** button compares every slide and question in Firestore against the application code and reports any discrepancies.

**What it checks:**
- Slides or questions missing from Firestore
- Fields that differ between Firestore and the code
- Items in Firestore that no longer exist in the code

**Reading the results:**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| All data matches | Firestore is fully in sync — no action needed |
| Field mismatch | A record exists but one or more fields differ — click **Seed Data** to fix |
| Missing in Firestore | A record in the code has not been pushed yet — click **Seed Data** to fix |

## 11. Managing Learning Slides

Navigate to **Slides** from the admin sidebar.

### 11.1 Slide List

All slides are listed in a table showing the slide title, category, and number of morphology cue markers. Use the **search bar** to filter by title or the **category dropdown** to filter by category.

### 11.2 Adding a New Slide

1. Click **Add Slide** (top right)
2. Fill in the slide form:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Category** | Which of the five learning categories this slide belongs to |
| **Title** | Name of the cell type or condition (e.g. "Superficial Squamous Cells") |
| **Image** | Upload the Pap smear image (drag and drop or click to browse) |
| **Cue Placement** | Add interactive morphology markers directly on the image |
| **Cellular Features** | List of cytoplasm and cell border observations |
| **Nuclear Features** | List of nuclear size, chromatin, and N/C ratio observations |
| **Background Features** | List of smear background observations |
| **Diagnostic Notes** | Interpretation paragraph and key differentials |
| **Clinical Relevance** | Clinical significance of the finding |

3. Click **Save Slide**

### 11.3 Placing Morphology Cue Markers

Cue markers are the numbered interactive pins that appear on the slide image.

1. Upload the slide image first
2. In the **Cue Placement** section, click **Place Cue**
3. The button will pulse blue — click on the exact location on the image where you want the marker
4. A popup editor will appear — enter the cue **Title** and **Description** (the morphology explanation)
5. Click anywhere else on the image to close the popup
6. Repeat for each morphological feature you want to annotate
7. To delete a cue, click its number then click **Delete**

> You can add as many cues per slide as needed. There is no maximum.

### 11.4 Editing a Slide

Click the **pencil icon** next to any slide in the list. All fields including cue markers are editable. Click **Save Slide** to confirm changes.

### 11.5 Deleting a Slide

Click the **trash icon** next to the slide. A confirmation prompt will appear before deletion. Deletion is permanent.

> After adding, editing, or deleting slides, run **Seed Data** from the Dashboard to sync changes to Firestore.

## 12. Managing Quiz Questions

Navigate to **Questions** from the admin sidebar.

### 12.1 Question List

All questions are listed with their category and difficulty level (colour-coded: green = beginner, yellow = intermediate, red = advanced). Use the search bar or category filter to locate specific questions.

### 12.2 Adding a New Question

1. Click **Add Question** (top right)
2. Fill in the question form:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Category** | Which learning category this question belongs to |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced |
| **Image** | Optional — upload a Pap smear image for image-based questions |
| **Question** | The question text or clinical scenario |
| **Options** | Four answer choices |
| **Correct Answer** | Must match one of the four options exactly (copy and paste to be safe) |
| **Explanation** | Why the correct answer is right — shown to students after they answer |
| **Clinical Relevance** | Clinical significance — shown after each answer |
| **Key Features** | Morphological features to look for — shown in feedback |
| **Hints** | Guided clues shown when a student answers incorrectly |

3. Click **Save Question**

> **Important:** The Correct Answer field must match one of the four option texts exactly, including capitalisation. If it does not match, no option will be marked correct.

### 12.3 Editing a Question

Click the **pencil icon** next to any question. All fields are editable. Click **Save Question** to confirm.

### 12.4 Deleting a Question

Click the **trash icon** next to the question. Deletion is permanent.

> After adding, editing, or deleting questions, run **Seed Data** from the Dashboard to sync changes to Firestore.

## 13. Managing Users

Navigate to **Users** from the admin sidebar.

### 13.1 User List

All registered students are listed with their name, email, role, and registration date. Use the **search bar** to find a student by name or email. Click **Refresh** to reload the list with the latest data.

### 13.2 Viewing a Student's Progress

Click the **chevron (▾)** button on the right of any user row to expand their progress details:

- **Per-category progress** — slides completed and highest quiz score for each of the five categories
- **Total XP** earned
- **Achievement count**
- **Last active date**

### 13.3 Managing Admin Access

Click the **shield icon** next to a user to toggle their role between **student** and **admin**.

- A filled blue shield indicates the user currently has admin access
- A confirmation prompt will appear before any role change

> Use this to grant admin access to a co-administrator, or to remove access when no longer needed.
